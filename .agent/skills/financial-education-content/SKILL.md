# Financial Education Content Skill

Expert skill for creating effective financial literacy content tailored to Kenyan context and diverse financial literacy levels.

## Core Principles

### 1. Kenyan Context & Cultural Relevance

**Use Local Examples**
- Reference M-Pesa, Safaricom, Equity Bank, KCB, etc.
- Use Kenyan Shillings (KES) in all examples
- Include local financial products (chamas, SACCOs, mobile loans)
- Reference local economic realities (matatu costs, rent, school fees)

**Cultural Considerations**
- Respect for communal financial obligations (harambee, family support)
- Understanding of informal savings groups (chamas, table banking)
- Awareness of diaspora remittances
- Recognition of agricultural income patterns
- Urban vs rural financial differences

**Local Financial Landscape**
- Mobile money dominance (M-Pesa, Airtel Money)
- Digital lending apps (Tala, Branch, Fuliza)
- SACCOs vs traditional banks
- NSE (Nairobi Securities Exchange) investment options
- Government bonds and T-bills
- NHIF, NSSF obligations

### 2. Content Structure & Pedagogy

**Progressive Learning Levels**
1. **Beginner**: Basic budgeting, saving, avoiding debt traps
2. **Intermediate**: Emergency funds, investing basics, insurance
3. **Advanced**: Portfolio diversification, tax optimization, wealth building

**Multi-Format Content**

**Articles**
- Keep under 800 words for mobile reading
- Use simple language (avoid jargon or define it)
- Break into scannable sections with headers
- Include practical action steps
- Add relatable stories/case studies

**Videos**
- Length: 3-7 minutes for most topics
- Use Kenyan accents and settings
- Include Swahili/Sheng subtitles where appropriate
- Mobile-optimized resolution
- Clear, simple visuals

**Quizzes**
- 5-8 questions per quiz
- Mix multiple choice and true/false
- Immediate feedback with explanations
- Gamification (points, badges, streaks)
- Scenario-based questions

**Games**
- Financial simulation games (budgeting challenge)
- Interactive calculators as games
- Savings goal challenges
- Debt payoff races
- Investment portfolio builders

### 3. Key Topics to Cover

**Foundational Topics**
- Creating a budget (50/30/20 rule adapted for Kenya)
- Tracking spending (including M-Pesa statements)
- Building emergency funds (3-6 months expenses)
- Understanding interest rates (especially mobile loans)
- Avoiding debt traps (high-interest digital loans)

**Intermediate Topics**
- Types of bank accounts (savings, current, money market)
- Understanding credit scores (CRB listings)
- Insurance basics (health, life, property)
- Investing fundamentals (risk vs return)
- Retirement planning (NSSF, personal pension)
- Goal-based saving (house, car, education)

**Advanced Topics**
- Stock market investing (NSE)
- Mutual funds and unit trusts
- Real estate investment (REITs, land, rentals)
- Tax planning (KRA compliance, deductions)
- Side hustles and entrepreneurship
- Wealth preservation and estate planning

**Kenya-Specific Topics**
- M-Pesa money management
- Understanding Fuliza and overdraft costs
- SACCO vs bank accounts
- Government securities (T-bills, bonds)
- Understanding CRB and credit repair
- Avoiding pyramid schemes (common in Kenya)
- Sending/receiving diaspora remittances efficiently

### 4. Content Quality Standards

**Accuracy**
- Verify all financial statistics and rates
- Update content quarterly (interest rates change)
- Cite credible sources (CBK, KRA, NSE)
- Get expert review for complex topics
- Disclose when content is opinion vs fact

**Inclusivity**
- Content for all income levels
- Examples for salaried and self-employed
- Urban and rural considerations
- Various literacy levels (use visuals)
- Accessibility (screen reader friendly)

**Actionability**
- Every piece should have clear action steps
- Provide templates and tools
- Link to relevant calculators
- Include checklists
- Set realistic, achievable goals

**Engagement**
- Start with relatable scenario or question
- Use storytelling (case studies)
- Interactive elements (polls, quizzes)
- Encourage community discussion
- Celebrate small wins

### 5. Tone & Voice

**Friendly, Not Patronizing**
- "Let's learn together" not "You don't know this"
- Acknowledge financial stress is real
- Celebrate progress, not perfection
- Use "we" language

**Practical, Not Academic**
- Simple explanations with examples
- Avoid financial jargon or explain it
- Focus on "why it matters to you"
- Real numbers and scenarios

**Empowering, Not Judgmental**
- No shame about past financial mistakes
- Focus on future improvement
- Recognize systemic barriers
- Highlight agency and control

### 6. Content Calendar Strategy

**Regular Cadence**
- Weekly articles (Monday motivation)
- Bi-weekly videos
- Daily tips/micro-content
- Monthly deep dives

**Seasonal Content**
- January: New Year financial goals
- April: Tax season (KRA deadlines)
- August: School fees planning
- December: Holiday budgeting, year-end review

**Trending Topics**
- Respond to current events (inflation, interest rates)
- New financial products launch
- Regulatory changes
- Economic news affecting Kenyans

## Content Creation Checklist

For every piece of content:

- [ ] Uses Kenyan context and examples
- [ ] Written in clear, simple language
- [ ] Includes practical action steps
- [ ] Mobile-friendly format
- [ ] Fact-checked and accurate
- [ ] Includes relevant visuals
- [ ] Links to related content/tools
- [ ] Has engagement element (quiz, discussion prompt)
- [ ] Optimized for SEO (if article)
- [ ] Accessibility checked

## Content Templates

### Article Structure
```
1. Hook (relatable scenario)
2. Problem statement
3. Why it matters
4. Solution/explanation (with examples)
5. Action steps
6. Related resources
7. Discussion prompt
```

### Video Script Structure
```
1. Intro (5-10 seconds, hook)
2. Main content (2-5 minutes, 3 key points)
3. Recap (30 seconds)
4. Call to action
5. Outro
```

### Quiz Structure
```
1. Context-setting intro
2. 5-8 scenario-based questions
3. Immediate feedback per question
4. Final score with encouragement
5. Recommended next content
```

## When to Use This Skill

Activate this skill when:
- Creating financial education articles, videos, or content
- Designing quizzes or interactive learning games
- Planning content calendar
- Reviewing content for accuracy and relevance
- Adapting financial concepts for Kenyan audience
- Ensuring cultural sensitivity and inclusivity
